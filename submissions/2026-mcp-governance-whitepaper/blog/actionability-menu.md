RANKED ACTIONABILITY MENU — what a leader can actually do with per-call attributable agent records joined to delivery data (cheap/Monday → big investment)

1. **The grant inventory ("would it survive?") audit.** One hour, one table: every workflow configuration, the credential it holds, the scope, the date granted, and the evidence column — what its output delivered since. Leverage: the evidence column comes back mostly blank, and the blank IS the finding; it converts the abstract thesis into a felt problem in their own org before they build anything. Fully self-run, tuned to whatever "evidence" means locally. **BLOG-light** (it's already the draft's closing line — currently a rhetorical question; making it a literal table with named columns is the upgrade).

2. **One credential per workflow configuration.** Stop sharing keys: issue each durable bundle (model + prompt + tools + policy) its own principal at the gateway. Leverage: the spec attributes to the authenticated principal, not to "the agent" — this convention is the thing that makes every later join possible; shared keys aggregate the whole fleet into attribution mush. It's also the honest, actionable rendering of the draft's "convention you build on top" line, which currently states the limit without handing the reader the move. **BLOG-light.**

3. **Run the join once, by hand.** Pick one workflow and one trailing month: pull its calls from the gateway log, pull the changes it opened from version control, and answer three questions per change — merged? reverted? rewritten? Spreadsheet or duckdb, no platform. Leverage: it discovers whether the org's data even joins (does the principal survive into the PR? does the trace context reach CI?) and it matches Zig's established register exactly — the goose tutorial's "do it once by hand and the black box becomes a list you wrote." **BLOG-light** — arguably the strongest possible blog move because it's the whole thesis at n=1.

4. **Publish tiers + a clock, sandbox default.** Two-to-four named autonomy tiers, published like the MCP SDK tiers, with a trailing window, defined relegation triggers, a review cadence (borrow the standard's four-week shape as a starting point), and every new/sparsely-observed workflow defaulting to a read-only sandbox tier enforced at the gateway. Bonus signal: a sandboxed workflow's *denied* calls are a free read on what scope it wants vs. needs. Leverage: it's the cadence as process-before-product — runnable in a spreadsheet day one, encodable in gateway policy later. **Split:** the "publish tiers, set a clock, default to sandbox" move is BLOG-light; *setting the dials* (window length vs. attribution lag, Goodhart-resistant signal choice, small-n cold start, unit-of-trust reasoning) is WHITEPAPER-deep and already lives in §5.

5. **Ban activity counts from grant decisions.** A one-paragraph policy: no scope review may cite calls made, PRs opened, or acceptance rate — only held-in-production outcome signals. Leverage: it's the Goodhart guard as a Monday memo; cheap, cultural, and it inoculates the org before a vendor dashboard teaches them the wrong metric. **BLOG-light** (one line) with the full justification WHITEPAPER-deep (§3's accuracy indictment).

6. **Relegation = revocation.** Make credential rotation part of the review ritual: a demoted workflow that keeps its old higher-tier key keeps the scope it was supposed to lose. Leverage: it's the operational detail that silently breaks every tier scheme, and almost nobody's key lifecycle is wired to their trust decisions. **WHITEPAPER-deep** (credential lifecycle design; §5 already carries the principle) — worth at most one sentence in the blog.

7. **Fleet-as-portfolio review.** Once the join runs, compute cost-to-outcome per workflow (tokens spent per merged-and-held change) and rank the fleet; refit or retire the configurations whose output the team keeps rewriting. Leverage: this is the actual answer to "are the agents paying off" — the CFO artifact — and it turns the join from governance plumbing into a budgeting instrument. **Split:** the portfolio *framing* is a strong blog beat (one paragraph); the measurement design (Evaluate's signal set, cost attribution, segmenting authorship) is WHITEPAPER-deep.

8. **Build the delivery evidence layer proper — and serve it back.** One store joining action records to VCS/CI events (Attribute/Evaluate), read by the review cadence AND served back to the agents as typed, cache-contracted operating context over MCP's own rails (Serve, via SEP-2106/2549), in the same budget cycle as the boundary work. Leverage: it's the difference between a quarterly spreadsheet ritual and infrastructure; sequencing enforcement first and evidence later reproduces the exact "perfectly recorded, unattributably valuable" fleet the argument opens on. **WHITEPAPER-deep** — architecture, not a blog move.
