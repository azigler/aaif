# AAIF Brand Guidelines

Use this skill whenever the user asks to create, review, or adapt content for AAIF, the Agentic AI Foundation. Apply these guidelines to visual design, slide decks, web pages, documents, blog graphics, social media assets, diagrams, event materials, and any other AAIF-branded deliverable.

## Brand Foundation

AAIF should feel neutral, collaborative, and trustworthy. The brand should communicate open governance, shared ownership, and credible infrastructure for the agentic AI ecosystem.

### Neutral

Governance designed so no single vendor controls the ecosystem. Decisions are made transparently, standards are developed collectively, and the work belongs to everyone.

Design and copy implications:

- Avoid visual treatments that imply ownership by one company, platform, or vendor.
- Use balanced layouts, clear hierarchy, and direct language.
- Favor open, transparent, standards-oriented framing.
- Avoid hype, exclusivity, or winner-takes-all positioning.

### Collaborative

Industry leaders working side by side toward a common purpose. Diverse perspectives and resources united to build what no single organization could own alone.

Design and copy implications:

- Emphasize cooperation, shared standards, ecosystem building, and cross-industry participation.
- Use inclusive language such as “together,” “shared,” “collective,” “open ecosystem,” and “community.”
- Avoid overly individualistic or hero-centric framing.

### Trustworthy

Built on the Linux Foundation’s reputation for neutrality and principled governance. The credibility and stability that open-source projects need to thrive.

Design and copy implications:

- Keep the tone confident, clear, and grounded.
- Prefer precise claims over broad hype.
- Use clean, professional compositions with strong contrast and readable typography.
- Avoid gimmicky, chaotic, or overly speculative AI aesthetics.

## Color Palette

### Primary Palette

- White: `#ffffff`
- Black: `#000000`

Use the primary palette as the foundation for most AAIF materials. Prioritize high contrast and clean compositions.

### Secondary Palette

- Orange: `#FF702D`
- Lavender: `#B6B0D0`

Use secondary colors as accents, emphasis, section markers, highlights, or graphic elements. Do not let secondary colors overwhelm the primary black-and-white foundation unless the asset explicitly calls for a bold campaign-style treatment.

## Typography

AAIF’s typographic system is built around **Instrument Sans**.

Use Instrument Sans for headings, body text, labels, captions, and UI text whenever available. If Instrument Sans is unavailable, choose a clean sans-serif fallback.

### Typographic Scale

The typographic scale is derived from doubling the root numbers 8, 9, 10, 12, and 16. The core natural progression is:

- `8`
- `16`
- `32`
- `64`
- `128`

Use this scale to create strong hierarchy. For example:

- Small labels or metadata: 8 or 16
- Body text or supporting text: 16 or 32, depending on medium
- Section headings: 32 or 64
- Hero titles or major campaign text: 64 or 128

For presentations, posters, and social graphics, use the larger end of the scale so text is bold, readable, and intentional.

## Visual Direction

When designing AAIF-branded materials:

1. Start with a black-and-white foundation.
2. Add orange or lavender only where it improves hierarchy, emphasis, or brand recognition.
3. Use Instrument Sans and the AAIF scale to create a confident typographic system.
4. Keep layouts clean, structured, and readable.
5. Communicate neutrality and trust through restraint, clarity, and balance.
6. Communicate collaboration through connected elements, multi-party framing, or inclusive copy.

## Copy Tone

AAIF copy should be:

- Clear
- Grounded
- Credible
- Open
- Collective
- Standards-oriented
- Vendor-neutral

Avoid:

- Breathless AI hype
- Claims that sound proprietary or vendor-owned
- “Magic” framing
- Fear-based messaging
- Overly casual jokes in official materials
- Dense jargon where plain language works

## Quick Checklist

Before finalizing AAIF-branded work, verify:

- The primary colors are `#ffffff` and `#000000`.
- Accent colors, if used, are `#FF702D` and/or `#B6B0D0`.
- Typography uses Instrument Sans or a close sans-serif fallback.
- Type sizes follow the 8 / 16 / 32 / 64 / 128 scale where practical.
- The content reflects AAIF as neutral, collaborative, and trustworthy.
- The logo, if needed, comes from the bundled `assets/` directory.
- The final result avoids vendor favoritism, hype, and generic AI visuals.
