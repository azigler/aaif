## Why it works (the moves to reuse)

- **A meme is the delivery vehicle.** A recognizable pop-culture still + the AAIF
  logo does the hook; the copy plays off the image. (Same shape as the Kirby
  -inhaling-food GIF for the MCP-stateless post.) The meme makes it shareable;
  the copy makes it land.
- **The dialogue/setup-payoff format** ("THEM: …— / ME: …? yea, i am 💅💄") — a
  playful flex framing borrowed from the meme.
- **Glam-irreverent emoji register** — 💅💄🙌💖. Femme, fun, confident. Distinct
  from generic 🚀💡✨ corporate emojis.
- **Lowercase, slangy, warm.** "they got me gal," "dev darlings," "faves~," "ty …
  for the select." Trailing `~` for sing-song energy.
- **Warm network tag as a PS.** Tagging Rizel Scarlett with a real reason ("more
  excuses for livestreams 💖") — networking artifact + genuine warmth, not a
  transactional credit.
- **Confident ownership, zero apology.** No "humbled to announce," no "obligatory
  plug." Just the flex, played for fun.

## The rule for AAIF posts

**Quirky wrapper, real payload.** The silliness (meme + glam emojis + lowercase
flex) is the vehicle; underneath it there should be something genuinely useful —
a teaching hook, a real insight, a promise to do the homework for the audience.
The acceptance post's payload was "here's what I'll be working on + a warm tag."
A spec post's payload is "here's what actually changes and whether you need to
care." Never silliness with nothing underneath; never a dry teach with no fun.

Calibration knobs: meme/GIF as hook · glam-irreverent emoji set · lowercase +
slang ("u," "ur," "w/," "da comments," "y'all") · warm tags w/ a real reason ·
one ALL-CAPS word or two for emphasis · close on a peer question. Keep the AAIF
framing community-warm and vendor-neutral (the LinearB tie stays a closing link).
